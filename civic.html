MUSWIT engages governments, embassies, and international organizations to create systemic change for women at the highest policy levels.

Overview

Diplomacy in Service of Women's Empowerment

MUSWIT's civic and diplomatic work is rooted in the conviction that lasting change requires systemic advocacy. We don't just serve women in our programs — we fight for the policies, funding, and international support that can transform the lives of millions.

Through formal partnerships, MOU agreements, and active participation in national and international forums, MUSWIT has become a recognized voice for Muslim women in civic and diplomatic spaces.

Partner With Us
Government Relations

Advocating at the National Level

We work with government ministries and agencies to ensure women's education and empowerment remain priority agenda items.

Ministry of Education

Formal MOU with the Ministry of Education providing MUSWIT access to schools, curriculum support, and national scholarship programs for girls.

Ministry of Gender & Social Welfare

Collaborative programs on gender-based violence prevention, economic empowerment, and social protection for vulnerable women.

National Youth & Employment Commission

Jointly designed vocational training initiatives that channel government funding into MUSWIT's TVET and ICT programs for women.

Embassy Engagements

International Diplomatic Partnerships

Our embassy relationships open doors to international funding, cross-border knowledge exchange, and global platforms for advocacy.

US Embassy Partnership

Annual collaboration on democracy, civic education, and women's leadership programs funded through embassy community grants.

British High Commission

Girls' education and digital skills initiatives supported through the British Council and FCDO development funding streams.

German Embassy / GIZ

Technical vocational education partnership with Deutsche Gesellschaft für Internationale Zusammenarbeit (GIZ) for quality TVET delivery.

International Organizations

Aligned With Global Movements for Change

MUSWIT's work is anchored in the UN Sustainable Development Goals and supported by key international development bodies.

UN Women

Aligned programming with UN Women's gender equality frameworks, and participation in international women's empowerment consultations.

UNESCO

Education quality partnerships and participation in UNESCO-led literacy and lifelong learning initiatives for women and girls.

UNICEF

Joint child and adolescent girl programs addressing school dropout, early marriage, and out-of-school youth re-engagement.

Corporate Partners

Private Sector Partners Driving Impact

We work with purpose-driven companies whose CSR commitments align with our mission of women's empowerment and community development.

Tech Sector Partners

Technology companies providing equipment, software licenses, trainer capacity, and internship opportunities for our ICT graduates.

Financial Institutions

Banks and microfinance institutions providing financial literacy training and seed grants for women entrepreneurs in our programs.

Manufacturing & Trade

Industry partners offering apprenticeship placements, raw material support, and market access for our TVET program graduates.
